01 — Foundation

Brand Strategy

PrimeNest Global is a direct-source Malaysian edible bird nest exporter operating owner-managed swiftlet houses in Kelantan since 2004. The brand serves international wholesale buyers, importers, luxury retailers, and corporate gifting clients across Asia, the Middle East, and Western markets.

01

Brand Promise

Traceable, handcrafted Malaysian bird nest — from our swiftlet houses to your door. No intermediaries. No ambiguity.

02

Brand Position

Premium direct-source exporter. Not a broker. Not a trader. The owner is the farmer. Every nest is traceable to its origin house.

03

Core Belief

Luxury begins at the source. Transparency is the ultimate premium signal in an industry built on opacity.

04

Brand Character

Quiet confidence. Restrained elegance. Operational precision. The kind of supplier that says less and proves more.

06 — Design System

Layout Principles

Every layout decision is a trust signal. Whitespace communicates confidence. Structure communicates reliability. Restraint communicates quality. These rules apply to every format — web, catalog, packaging insert, trade deck, or social post.

01

Grid First

Use a 12-column grid on all digital layouts. Use defined column gutters on all print layouts. Nothing floats without grid alignment.

02

Whitespace as Design

Margins are not wasted space. They are active design elements. Minimum page margin: 80px web, 20mm print. Never fill every inch.

03

Product as Hero

The product image is always the dominant visual. Text and graphic elements support it. Never compete with the product image.

04

Visual Hierarchy

One primary element per layout zone. One headline dominates. One CTA per section. Never create competing focal points.

05

Asymmetry Rule

Asymmetry is allowed only when visually balanced by mass, not by decoration. Editorial asymmetry: yes. Random placement: never.

06

Consistency Lock

Every new layout must reference an approved layout. Component spacing, image ratios, and type sizes are not re-decided per project.

Button Rules

All buttons use sharp edges (no border-radius). Consistent height: 48px. Font: Inter 500, 11–12px, +0.14em tracking, all-caps. Never rounded pill buttons.

Form Rules

Bottom-border-only inputs. No box outlines. Label above field, 10px uppercase, tracked. Maximum 6 fields per form. No CAPTCHA visible to user.

Navigation Rules

Maximum 5 primary nav items. No dropdown mega-menus. Mobile: slide-in panel, never hamburger with accordion. No sticky CTA bars.

Prohibited UI Patterns

No countdown timers. No popups within 10 seconds. No "SALE" banners. No chat widgets over product images. No autoplaying video with audio.

08 — Design System

Motion System

Luxury brands move slowly. Every transition communicates brand character. Fast, jarring, or bouncy animations signal low quality. Slow, deliberate, and soft motion signals confidence.

800ms

Page Transitions

Fade in from opacity 0. Ease-out cubic-bezier. No slides, no scales, no bounces. Pure dissolve.

400ms

UI Interactions

Hover states, button transitions, link underlines. Ease-out. Never bounce. Never spring.

1200ms

Hero Reveals

Text fade + subtle upward drift (12px). Staggered by 160ms per element. Ease-out cubic.

2400ms

Video / Reel Pacing

Minimum clip length for social video. Slow pans, long dissolves, no jump cuts. Product breathes on screen.

✓ Motion Allowed
  • Slow opacity fades between scenes
  • Subtle Ken Burns on still photography
  • Gentle parallax scrolling (max 20% offset)
  • Clean crossfades in video
  • Smooth scroll-triggered text reveals
✕ Motion Prohibited
  • TikTok-speed fast cuts
  • Zoom-in punch transitions
  • Rotating, spinning, or bouncing elements
  • Glitch effects or distortion transitions
  • Music-sync beat-drop editing

10 — Production

Photography Art Direction

Photography is PrimeNest's most powerful trust asset. Every image must pass as editorial — magazine-ready without heavy post-production. The product is always the truth, and the photography proves it.

Lighting Direction

Soft directional natural light from one side. Window light or diffused studio strobe. Warm colour temperature: 4500–5500K. No direct flash. No harsh shadows. Subtle fill only.

Background Rules

Warm white, soft ivory, or matte stone/linen surfaces only. Never pure white (#FFFFFF) — it reads clinical. Never busy patterns. Keep background colour within the brand palette.

Camera Settings

Minimum 42MP for packshots. Macro lens for texture detail shots. Shallow depth of field allowed only for lifestyle context — never for hero product shots.

Post-Processing

Minimal retouching. Colour grade toward warm-neutral. No HDR. No aggressive clarity or texture sliders. The nest should look as it does in hand.

Bird Nest Product Photography — Hard Rules

✓ Correct Nest Representation
  • Natural ivory or cream-beige color tone
  • Visible woven fiber texture — real strands
  • Real handcrafted imperfections and variations
  • Accurate cup and boat nest shapes
  • Semi-translucent fibers visible in light
  • Natural feather traces where appropriate
  • Slight natural discoloration is authentic — keep it
✕ Never Represent Nests As
  • Pure white or over-bleached appearance
  • Glowing or luminous product renders
  • Plastic or polished smooth texture
  • Fluffy or cotton-like appearance
  • Fake CGI or 3D-rendered nests
  • Stacked in unrealistic perfect pyramids
  • Surrounded by Chinese herbal props

Packshot Type

Isolated product on clean ivory surface. Straight-on and 45° angles. Consistent lighting rig across all SKUs so catalog feels uniform.

Lifestyle Type

Product in minimal luxury context: ceramic bowl, natural linen, wooden surface. One prop maximum. Never use props that reference traditional medicine.

Operational Type

Farm, harvest, processing, cleaning. Shot on-site. Natural available light preferred. Authenticity over polish — but composition must remain intentional.

13 — Production

Content Hierarchy

Every sales material — website page, catalog spread, ad creative, social post, or presentation slide — follows the same six-step messaging architecture. This ensures buyers receive information in the order that builds trust.

01
Product Beauty
Lead with the product image. Large, clean, hero-scale. No text competing. Let the product speak before any claim is made. This is the emotional entry point.
02
Source Transparency
Who we are. Where it comes from. Owner-operated. Kelantan since 2004. This is the trust anchor that separates PrimeNest from traders and brokers.
03
Product Specifications
Grade, form, weight, moisture content, shape classification, origin type. Clean table or structured copy. Buyers need this to make purchasing decisions. Be precise.
04
Export Capability
Monthly supply volume. Lead time. Documentation available. Customs compliance. Packaging options. This converts a qualified buyer into an active inquiry.
05
Trust Signals
Lab testing. Phytosanitary certificates. Export documentation. Farm photos. Real team. Traceable lot codes. Never generic claims — always specific, verifiable proof.
06
Inquiry Action
One clear, low-friction next step. WhatsApp, email, or inquiry form. No hard sell language. "Begin your inquiry" or "Request wholesale pricing" — never "Buy Now".

14 — Messaging

Tone of Voice

Visual branding collapses the moment copy sounds wrong. Every word must pass the same restraint test as every design decision. PrimeNest speaks with the confidence of a company that does not need to convince — it simply shows.

✓ Write Like This
"Every nest traced to a single swiftlet house in Kelantan. Nothing more."
✕ Never Write Like This
"The BEST QUALITY Bird Nest with the HIGHEST GRADE at UNBEATABLE wholesale prices!!!"
✓ Write Like This
"Our monthly supply capacity is consistent. We do not over-promise."
✕ Never Write Like This
"Unlimited stock available! Fast shipping worldwide! Best bird nest supplier in Malaysia!"
✓ Write Like This
"Lab-certified. Export-documented. Origin-traceable. Available for qualified importers."
✕ Never Write Like This
"100% natural! Boosts immunity! Anti-aging superfood from ancient Chinese tradition!"
AttributeWhat It Means in Practice
ConfidentState facts directly. No hedging. "Our swiftlet houses" not "our possibly owner-operated facilities."
CleanShort sentences. No filler. Remove every word that does not add meaning.
PreciseSpecific numbers over vague claims. "100kg per month minimum supply" beats "large quantity available."
GlobalEnglish first. Chinese always available. No regional slang. No informal abbreviations in formal copy.
TransparentName the farm. Name the region. Show the process. The more specific, the more trust.
PremiumNever mention price in headlines. Never use "cheap," "affordable," or "discount." Pricing is private and professional.

15 — Messaging

Trust Asset System

Luxury alone does not close export deals. Sophisticated buyers require operational proof. Trust assets are the evidence layer that converts brand interest into purchase orders. Every asset must be real, specific, and documented.

Farm Ownership Proof

Photography of named swiftlet houses. GPS-tagged location reference. Owner present in operational imagery. Named, not anonymous — this is the primary differentiator from brokers.

Processing Transparency

Photo and video of the cleaning and sorting process. Show the hands. Show the tools. Show the environment. Real operational imagery beats any marketing render.

Traceability System

Each export batch carries a lot code traceable to harvest date, house location, and processing batch. Document this clearly. Build a QR-linked traceability page.

Export Documentation

Phytosanitary certificates. Health certificates. Certificate of Origin. Free Sale Certificate. COA (Certificate of Analysis). Keep templates clean and visually branded.

Lab Testing

Third-party lab test reports. Heavy metal, microbial, moisture content. Available on request, or summary published on website. Never make health claims not supported by documentation.

Supply Capacity Statement

Publish realistic monthly supply figures. Buyers need to qualify volume before engaging. "100kg+ per month available" is a trust signal. Ambiguity is a red flag to serious importers.
